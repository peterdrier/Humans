## Question

I'm on the Board. How does voting on a Colaborador application actually work?

## Answer

Here's how the Board voting process works for Colaborador applications:

## Submitting Votes

1. A human submits a Colaborador application.
2. Each Board member casts **one vote per application** — you each get exactly one vote.
3. Votes are visible to Board members in the application's voting detail view.

## Finalizing the Decision

A Board or Admin member approves or rejects the application with:
- A **collective decision note**
- A **Board meeting date**

## What Happens on Approval

- The human's membership tier is updated to Colaborador on their profile.
- They are added to the **Colaboradors system team**.
- Their term expiry is set to the **next December 31 of an odd year that is at least 2 years from the approval date** (e.g. if approved in 2025, the term expires 31 Dec 2027).

## Vote Record Privacy (GDPR)

Once the application is finalized (approved or rejected), **all individual Board vote records are deleted**. Only the collective decision note and meeting date are retained. This is intentional data minimization.

## Prerequisites

For a **Colaborador** application specifically, the human must already be an active Volunteer — but there's no requirement to hold any higher tier first (that's only needed for Asociado applications, which require being an approved Colaborador).
